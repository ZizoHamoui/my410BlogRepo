Week 04 Jan 29, 2024

Entry 2: GDD & Smart Depth

This week was all about structure and mechanics. As we moved into the Game Design Document (GDD) phase, I wanted to ensure the team wasn't just working hard, but working safely. When I set the agenda and structure, I intentionally built in a "risk factor"—setting our internal deadlines a few days earlier than the actual due date. This buffer proved immediately useful; when the narrative flowboard needed last-minute adjustments, I was able to jump in and refine it without putting us behind schedule.

On the design side, my focus was strictly on defining our "Smart Depth." I wanted mechanics that felt distinct rather than repetitive, so I conceptualized three core pillars:
1. Mug Blocks: Creating environmental pressure.
2. Freeze Status: A capability boost for the player.
3. Tower Upgrades: Providing strategic choice.
Once I established this logic, Rakko jumped in to flesh out the specific values, which was a great collaborative workflow.

For the presentation, I assigned slides based on ownership. By having team members present the specific parts they worked on, I noticed everyone felt much more confident and eager to speak.

Reflection

Looking ahead, I am shifting back from "Manager" mode to "Developer" mode. I'll admit, I'm feeling a little nervous about the technical workload. It’s been a while since I was in a flow state with Unity, so I’ve blocked out two full days this weekend to just code and resolve that rust.

Team-wise, the energy is great—everyone has really absorbed the vision and is excited. The only hurdle right now is communication; occasionally the language barrier creates small friction points, but given the team's attitude, I know this is something we can easily overcome.
