Week 04 Jan 29, 2024

Entry 2

This week was all about getting our ideas down on paper, or rather, figJam board. We shifted gears to focus on the Game Design Document (GDD), and I wanted to make sure we had a solid foundation before diving into the details. I started by setting up the agenda and document structure so the rest of the team could easily jump in and add their parts without us stepping on each other's toes.

I also spent a lot of time refining the mechanics, finalizing our narrative flowboards, and defining exactly how our gameplay loops and resolution types will function. It felt good to get those specific details locked in. To wrap things up, I built our slide deck and assigned speaking roles.

Now that the design is settled, I’m jumping back into Unity. Next week, I will start tackling the core mechanics; getting the tiling system and drag-and-drop towers actually working. It’s going to be a heavy coding week, but I’m excited to see how this comes along.

Week 02 Jan 15, 2024

Entry 1

This week was an intense start to our IAT 410 project, Desk TD, as we prepared for the Greenlight Review. As Project Manager and Lead Programmer, I took the lead on our initial ideation sessions using Figjam to organize our tower-defense concepts.

My primary contribution was drafting our idea for the game proposal and tightening it on FigJam. I spent significant time defining our core mechanics, such as the "Inked Tile" splat system and the "Spark" economy, to ensure our design was both technically feasible. To help my team visualize the project, I created a detailed storyboard on Figjam, which served as a reference for Janis while she designed our stationery characters and refined stroyboards.

On the management side, I coordinated task distribution and handled the final submission of our documentation. Unfortunately, a sudden bout of food poisoning prevented me from attending the in-person presentation. I had to quickly pivot, delegating my slides on "Mechanics and Justification" to Rakko and Kate to ensure the team was supported during the pitch.

Looking ahead, I am beginning to set up our Unity environment for us to be able to start working from the next week.
